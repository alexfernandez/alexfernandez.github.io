How much would 0.2% of mirror surfaces affect our temperature? Or put another way, how much incoming heat can we reflect back to space, p(heat)? Sadly, it is too optimistic to expect that we can reflect 0.2% of heat. Our mirrors will not be perfect across all wavelengths. ğŸ’Š Side Effects

The geopolitical aspects of our little plan are messy. Getting an international agreement for roof mirrors would probably not be easy, and the logistics of wrapping all buildings in tinfoil can also be tricky.

The nice part of this project is that infrared mirrors on roofs would help cool our buildings, reducing the need for air conditioning. The same is true for windows by the way. Whole cities might also be cooler this way. Even for cold countries this effect would be welcome in summer. But globally it would hardly make a dent.
